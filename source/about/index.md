# About me

Hi, I am a Deep Learning Framework Engineer at Nvidia. I earned a PhD degree from University of New Mexico, US. in electrical engineering and minor in applied mathematics. Go Lobos!

## Backgrounds

I work on supporting the latest NVIDIA technologies for deep learning compilers and inference frameworks, including XLA/JAX, SGLang, and vLLM. Previously, I studied computational electromagnetics under the guidance of Prof. [Zhen Peng](https://acem.ece.illinois.edu/) and Prof. [Christos Christodoulou](http://www.ece.unm.edu/faculty-staff/electrical-and-computer/christos-christodoulou.html). My research focuses on parallel computing and numerical methods for partial differential equations. Before PhD programm, I spent seven years studying optics, which built a strong foundation in wave physics and modeling. Outside of research, I enjoy playing the guitar as a form of personal expression and relaxation. I value rational thinking and have a deep appreciation for mathematics as a way to understand the world.
